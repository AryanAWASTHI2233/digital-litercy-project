 ✅ Responsible Social Media Use
A Checkpoint Guide for College Students

🟢 DO's — Check These Off
☐ Checkpoint 1 — Pause Before You Post
Got something ready to share? Stop for just a second. Ask yourself — "Does this actually represent me well?" If it feels even slightly off, sit on it. Your gut is usually right.
☐ Checkpoint 2 — Lock It Down
Strong password? ✔ Privacy settings reviewed? ✔ Two-factor authentication on? ✔ Five minutes of setup today can save you a serious mess later.
☐ Checkpoint 3 — Make It Worth Sharing
Before posting, ask — "Does this add anything?" Something useful, something real, something that makes someone smile or think. If it adds value, share it. If it's just noise, maybe skip it.
☐ Checkpoint 4 — Think Long-Term
That post you're about to make — imagine a future employer seeing it three years from now. Still comfortable? Great. Not so much? You have your answer.
☐ Checkpoint 5 — Use It Smart
Follow people who challenge and inspire you. Join academic communities. Explore career content. Social media can genuinely open doors — if you point it in the right direction.

🔴 DON'Ts — Warning Flags to Watch
☐ Checkpoint 6 — Guard Your Personal Info
Your address, phone number, and passwords are not for public consumption — ever. If a post includes personal details, take it out before you share.
☐ Checkpoint 7 — Don't Take the Bait
Someone being aggressive or offensive online? Don't engage. No argument on the internet has ever truly been "won." Mute, block, and move on — your peace is worth more.
☐ Checkpoint 8 — Verify Before You Share
Seen something shocking or hard to believe? Good — be suspicious of it. Take 60 seconds to check the source before forwarding. Spreading misinformation, even accidentally, sticks to your name.
☐ Checkpoint 9 — Watch the Clock
Opened your phone for "just a minute" and lost an hour? We've all been there. Set a daily limit, use app timers, or simply put it face-down when you study. Your grades will notice the difference.
☐ Checkpoint 10 — The Final Check
Before anything goes live — would this hurt your reputation? Could it hurt someone else? If there's even a small "maybe" to either question, don't post it. No like or share is worth that cost.
