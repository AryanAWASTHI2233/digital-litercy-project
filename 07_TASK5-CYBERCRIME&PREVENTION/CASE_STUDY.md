**Case Study Title: Identity Theft in the Digital Age**


** Introduction : **

Identity theft is a serious cybercrime where someone’s 
personal information is stolen and used without their
permission. In today’s digital world, where people rely 
heavily on online banking, shopping, and social media, 
such crimes are becoming more common and dangerous.


** Background of the Case : **

Ravi Sharma, a college student, regularly used online platforms
for daily tasks like paying bills and shopping. One day, he 
received an email that looked exactly like it was from his
bank, asking him to update his account details to avoid any 
issues. Without suspecting anything, Ravi clicked on the link
and entered his login details and OTP.


** Modus Operandi (How the Crime Occurred) : **

The email was actually sent by a cybercriminal
who had created a fake website that looked identical
to the bank’s official site. Once Ravi entered his
details, the attacker captured them instantly. The criminal
then logged into Ravi’s account, changed his password, and 
gained complete access. Within a short time, money was transferred, 
and even a loan was taken in Ravi’s name.


** Target Group : **

Such attacks usually target students, elderly people, 
and anyone who may not verify online messages carefully.
Even regular users can become victims if they act in a hurry.


** Consequences and Impact : **

Ravi lost money and had to go through a stressful and 
time-consuming process to recover his account. His credit 
score was also affected, causing further problems.


** Conclusion : **

This case shows how important it is to stay alert online. 
Simple precautions can help prevent serious losses.
